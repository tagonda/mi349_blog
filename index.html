The Greatest Chili of All Time

By Tess Gonda | September 22, 2025

Ingredients:

  • 2 tbsp. olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 1 40oz can of Brooks hot chili beans
  • 1 15.5oz can of Brooks mild chili beans
  • 2 15oz cans of black beans (or whatever kind you prefer!)
  • 2 15oz cans of chopped/crushed/diced tomatoes
  • 1 8oz can of tomato sauce
  • 1 4oz can of mild chopped green chiles
  • 3 tbsp. chili powder
  • 2 tbsp. cumin
  • 1/2 tsp. basil
  • 1/4 tsp. ground coriander

    • As we approach fall (though it certainly doesn't feel like it in this weather), I have the perfect recipe for the season. I apologize in advance to my mother, seeing as this is her famous recipe... However, it is so delicious that I must share it on my incredibly successful and popular blog. This chili is perfect for a cozy day in, especially if you are a vegetarian! This recipe has no meat in it, though I'm sure it would taste great if you added some.

    Heat up the olive oil in a large pot. Add the chopped onion, and cook until translucent. Once the onions reach translucency, add in the chopped garlic and cook until both are golden brown. Add all cans of beans to the pot without draining any of the liquid and mix. Add in the cans of chopped/crushed/diced tomatoes (whichever you choose), the can of tomato sauce, and then use the same 8oz tomato sauce can to pour in two cans of water. Throw in the seasonings, adjusting as necessary. When I make this, I typically use more seasonings than the recipe calls for, but taste as you go. Add salt and pepper if needed. Bring the pot to a boil, then reduce to a simmer. I like to simmer mine for a few hours, but it still tastes great if you don't. Top with cheese, cilantro, and green onion.

    This recipe makes a ton of chili, so it's perfect for leftovers. I like to freeze mine in individual containers so I can grab one whenever I want. It also tastes great with tortilla chips. I know that isn't the greatest photo, but it tastes amazing. Enjoy!

    My Top 10 Most Expensive Pokémon Cards

    Card Name Variant Year Market Price
    Dark Dragonite Expansion Stamp Holo 2004 $242.00
    Blastoise Normal Holo 1999 $159.00
    Rayquaza Normal Holo 2006 $118.00

    The table above lists my top 10 most expensive Pokémon cards. It may not seem too crazy, but I am also a college student who doesn't have a ton of money, so I'm pretty proud of my collection. I track my collection on the TCG Collector website, and in total my cards are worth around $4457! I haven't logged the most recent cards that I've gotten, though I have a little over 1450 individual cards altogether.

    I'm a big fan of the older cards, mainly in the 1999-2009 range. I have collected almost the entirety (102 cards) of the first ever English Pokémon set, known as "Base Set", and I'm only missing one card: The holo Charizard. This card goes for a few hundred dollars, regardless of the condition. It could be torn, bent, or scratched, but still never be under $100. One day I will get my hands on it...
